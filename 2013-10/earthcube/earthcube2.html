What is EarthCube?

Formed as a collaborative partnership between two National Science Foundation Directorates, EarthCube is a large-scale effort to transform the conduct of geosciences research with a community-driven data and knowledge management system to address scientific grand challenges while promoting cross-disciplinary collaboration.

Achieving Success

EarthCube's success depends equally on the successful development of technical and social systems across numerous disciplines. Simply put, a robust, agile technical system depends on an equally robust and agile social system. In order to achieve this vision, EarthCube is leveraging broad community participation in all aspects of the development process.

Get Involved!

EarthCube is a virtual community with 1550+ participants. EarthCube is carried out in the groups, blogs, discussion posts, and at the virtual and in-person meetings of the EarthCube groups across the country, and at EarthCube events at professional conferences, including AGU, AMS, ASLO, ESIP, GSA, OGC, RDAP, and others.
